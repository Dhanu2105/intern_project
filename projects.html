FADEN-CNN
Model based breath pattern recognition

The Wireless Flexible Electronic Nasal Sensor

The Wireless Flexible Electronic Nasal (WIFLEN) sensor, combined with a Fast Dense CNN (FASDEN-CNN), captures temperature, pressure, and humidity at the nostrils to recognize breathing patterns. Data was collected during various activities and cleaned to remove outliers. The process included feature extraction, statistical analysis, and developing a deep AI model for classification. Inhalation and exhalation points were identified to calculate respiratory metrics, validated against a clinical device. Notable differences in nostril measurements were found across activities, and Hilbert transform was used to create 2D spectrograms for binary classification with the FASDEN-CNN.Know More →

EDRS-LSTM
Performance Evaluation of Nasal Sensor

Respiratory Signal Assessment Framework

The framework for collecting and assessing respiratory signals involves a wearable nasal mask that captures temperature, pressure, and humidity variations from both nostrils during normal breathing, deep breathing, and walking. Unlike traditional thoracic sensors, this method uses a nasal sensor for data recording. The collected signals were cleansed and segmented using a Hamming window of 10 seconds with 50% overlap. The normalized segments were then analyzed using the EDRS-LSTM deep learning model. The framework's performance was evaluated based on precision, recall, ROC, accuracy, and F1 score, focusing on the three breathing conditions. Know More →

HUMIDITY SENSOR
Continuous respiratory assessment

Evaluation Framework for Respiratory Signals

The evaluation framework for respiratory signals focuses on four breathing patterns: normal, deep, rapid, and shallow breathing during recovery. A wearable nasal mask with humidity sensors detects fluctuations from both nostrils. The raw data undergoes baseline drift removal and outlier elimination using Inter Quartile Range (IQR) techniques. To address the non-stationary nature of the signals, the data is normalized and segmented with 80% overlap. An ensemble model combining 1D-CNN, LSTM, and SVM classifiers is used for analysis and categorization.
