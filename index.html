Wireless Flexible Electric Breathing Assessment Monitor

Advancing respiratory health through wearable nasal sensor technology.

About

AAVISHKAR WIFLEN is an innovative, next-generation smart wearable designed for continuous, real-time analysis of breathing patterns and extraction of critical respiratory vitals. Compact, wireless, and user-friendly, it combines advanced sensing technology, artificial intelligence, and ergonomic design to deliver clinical-grade respiratory monitoring in both medical and everyday settings. Built on a flexible printed circuit board (FPCB), WIFLEN integrates nano-material and MEMS-based multiparameter sensors capable of simultaneously measuring temperature, pressure, and humidity. This unique combination enhances sensitivity, precision, and contextual understanding of respiratory activity, enabling accurate detection of changes in airflow, breathing rhythm, and environmental influence. The lightweight, battery-powered design ensures comfort and mobility, allowing users to wear the device unobtrusively during daily activities.

WIFLEN connects wirelessly to an integrated mobile application, which employs machine learning algorithms to analyze the captured data, identify respiratory patterns, and generate automated clinical markers. These AI-driven insights assist healthcare professionals in diagnosis and monitoring, while empowering users with personalized respiratory health information and trend tracking.

Designed with modularity at its core, WIFLEN can be customized for diverse applications. In healthcare, it supports continuous respiratory assessment for patients with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), asthma, sleep apnea, or post-surgical recovery. In sports and fitness, it helps athletes monitor breathing efficiency and optimize performance. In wellness and mental health, it aids stress management through respiratory pattern analysis. By merging portability, precision sensing, and data intelligence, AAVISHKAR WIFLEN bridges the gap between hospital-based monitoring and real-world usability.

Respiratory Signals Evaluation

This project aims at developing a wireless nasal mask that effectively monitors breathing patterns by measuring moisture from your breath. This mask identifies four main breathing patterns: normal, deep, rapid, and shallow. It works by collecting data through sensors, cleaning the data, and organizing it for analysis. The project uses advanced techniques like 1D-CNN, LSTM, and SVM. System performance is evaluated using Accuracy and MCC. This mask can help healthcare professionals detect respiratory problems early.
